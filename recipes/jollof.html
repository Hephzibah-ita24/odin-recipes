Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a popular West African dish made by cooking rice in a flavorful tomato-based sauce with onions, peppers, and a blend of spices.

It is commonly served with chicken, beef, fish, or fried plantains and is a centerpiece at family meals and celebrations.


Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Red bell pepper
  • Scotch bonnet or chili peppers
  • Onions
  • Vegetable oil
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Chicken stock
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Curry powder
  • Dried thyme
  • Bay leaves

Steps

  1. Blend the tomatoes, one onion, red bell peppers, and Scotch bonnet peppers until smooth.
  2. Wash the rice thoroughly. If using regular long-grain rice, parboil it for about 8–10 minutes, then drain. If using parboiled rice, simply rinse well.
  3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot. Fry the tomato paste for about 3–5 minutes, stirring frequently until it becomes darker and less acidic.
  4. Add the sliced onion and cook for 2 minutes. Pour in the blended pepper mixture and cook over medium heat for 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until most of the liquid evaporates and the oil begins to separate.
  5. Season the sauce by adding curry powder,thyme, bay leaves,seasoning cubes ,salt, pepper and stir well
  6. Pour in the chicken or beef stock and bring it to a gentle boil. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
  7. Pour in the chicken or beef stock and bring it to a gentle boil. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
  8. Cover the pot tightly. For the best results, place a sheet of aluminum foil or parchment paper under the lid to trap steam. Cook on low heat for 25–35 minutes, checking occasionally and adding a splash of hot water if the rice becomes too dry before it's fully cooked.
  9. Remove the bay leaves, fluff the rice with a fork, and let it rest for 5 minutes before serving.
